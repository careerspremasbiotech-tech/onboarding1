Ethics & Compliance

Code of Conduct & Business Ethics

This is mandatory reading. Every Premas employee must understand and live by the Code. It covers how we work, how we treat each other, and the ethical standards we hold ourselves to — everywhere we operate.

A Message From Our MD
"I want you all to be proud to work for Premas Biotech — not just because we make groundbreaking advancements for a better world, but because we do so to the highest ethical standards. It is the responsibility of every colleague to read and make sure that they understand the Code of Conduct."
— Dr. Prabuddha Kundu, Managing Director, Premas Biotech
Why the Code Exists: Our Code of Conduct exists to protect both you and the organisation — providing clear guidance on acceptable behaviour and preventing anyone from facing difficult or avoidable situations due to a lack of clarity. Non-compliance can have serious consequences for both Premas and you personally — including legal penalties, fines, and personal repercussions.

The Ethical Dilemma Test

If you are ever unsure whether an action is appropriate, ask yourself these four questions before proceeding:

⚖️

Is it legal?

Does the action comply with all applicable laws in India and in any other jurisdiction involved?

📋

Is it consistent with our Code?

Does it align with the principles, policies, and values set out in this Code of Conduct?

📰

Would I be comfortable seeing it reported?

If this action appeared in a newspaper or was shared externally — would you be comfortable with that?

👥

Can I justify it to others?

Could you explain and defend this decision clearly to colleagues, your family, or friends?

If the answer to any of these is "no" or "I'm not sure" — stop. Seek advice from your line manager or HR before proceeding.
Section 1

Respect for Our People

Our people are our greatest asset. It is essential that everyone at Premas can thrive and perform at their best. This requires respecting laws, maintaining optimal working conditions, and treating each other with dignity and respect — no matter where we operate.

R·1 Zero Tolerance for Bullying & Harassment ▼

Bullying or harassment of any kind — including on social media — is strictly prohibited. Every employee has the right to work without fear.

  • Do not discriminate against anyone for any reason — gender, caste, religion, disability, age, or any other characteristic
  • Do not bully, intimidate, or harass colleagues — in person, in writing, or digitally
  • If you are a victim or witness to bullying or harassment, report it to your line manager, HR, or through the anonymous reporting process
  • All reports will be handled confidentially and investigated thoroughly
  • Retaliation against someone who raises a concern in good faith is itself a disciplinary offence
POSH Act Compliance: Premas complies fully with the Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013. The POSH Policy is available on the company intranet. A POSH Committee is constituted as required by law.
R·2 Culture of Excellence & Fair Treatment ▼
  • Fair Decision-Making: Recruitment, compensation, development, and promotion decisions must be based solely on ability, work performance, experience, and demonstrated potential
  • Diversity and Development: We support the development of all talent within the organisation — irrespective of background
  • Respect and Dignity: Treat everyone with integrity, honesty, courtesy, and respect at all times
  • Labour Law: Premas complies with all applicable employment laws. Where there is a conflict between local laws and Premas policy, the stricter rule applies
Section 2

Compliance, Integrity & Financial Controls

C·1 Anti-Bribery & Corruption — Zero Tolerance ▼

Premas has a strict zero-tolerance policy against bribery and corruption in any form. Breaking anti-bribery laws can result in severe penalties for the company and for individuals personally — including unlimited fines and imprisonment.

  • Do not demand or accept money, gifts, or services from suppliers, dealers, or business partners in return for any favour
  • Do not offer bribes or kickbacks to public officials or commercial partners
  • Do not make facilitation payments — payments to expedite processes that would occur regardless — in any country
  • When interacting with government officials: never engage in actions that inappropriately influence their duties
If you suspect bribery or corruption — in any form — report it immediately to your line manager, Head HR, or through the anonymous reporting portal.
C·2 Gifts & Hospitality — Rules and Prohibitions ▼

Gifts and hospitality can build legitimate business relationships. However, they must never influence — or appear to influence — impartial decision-making.

You must NOT offer any gift or hospitality:
  • To any public employee or government official — strictly prohibited
  • That violates the recipient's own organisation's policies
  • That could be regarded as illegal, improper, or that implies a return favour is expected
  • In the form of cash or cash equivalents (gift vouchers, tokens, etc.)
  • That is extravagant or overly lavish — hospitality must be suitable for the purpose of the meeting
  • To anyone on the company's exclusion list — check with BD or HR if unsure
C·3 Conflicts of Interest ▼

A conflict of interest arises when your personal interests could — or appear to — interfere with your professional duties at Premas. Conflicts can occur naturally and are not automatically a violation; the obligation is to disclose and manage them transparently.

Examples of potential conflicts:
  • Owning shares or part-ownership of a Premas supplier, customer, or competitor
  • Working (paid or unpaid) for a competitor, supplier, or customer
  • Involving family members or friends in Premas business transactions
  • Accepting inducements that could influence your decision-making
What to do:
  • Report any actual or potential conflict of interest to your line manager or HR immediately
  • Do not take any action related to the conflict before it is formally resolved
  • Your manager may consult Head HR and the Ethics process for guidance
C·4 Financial Integrity & Accurate Records ▼
  • All business records — whether for internal use or external reporting — must be truthful, accurate, and complete
  • No business record should ever be falsified, misstated, or made misleading by omitting material facts — this is a zero-tolerance offence
  • Expenses must be authorised by your line manager. Do not authorise anything that results in a personal benefit to you
  • Report any suspected financial crime or fraud to your line manager, Head HR, or through the Speak Up process immediately
C·5 Charitable & Political Donations ▼
  • Charitable donations must be made only to legitimate organisations whose goals align with Premas values — and must not create a conflict of interest
  • Premas does not make donations to support any political party or candidate — in cash or in kind
  • Personal political or community activities are your own choice — but must be conducted outside working hours and without using Premas resources (email, letterhead, etc.)
Section 3

Protecting Assets & Information Security

A·1 Protecting Company Assets — Physical & Intellectual ▼

It is everyone's responsibility to protect Premas' assets — financial, intellectual, and physical. This includes IT systems, laboratory equipment, data, IP, facilities, and company funds.

  • Use company resources for work-related activities only
  • Report any misuse, theft, damage, or fraud to your line manager or Head HR immediately
  • Do not remove company property from the premises without authorisation
A·2 Information Security — Your Obligations ▼
Do:
  • Report anything suspicious immediately — emails, phone calls, unusual network activity
  • Complete all mandatory annual IT security training
  • Contact the IT/InfoSec team early if you're introducing any new technology with a security element
  • Use only company-approved systems for all Premas business
Do NOT:
  • Use company systems for illegal, fraudulent, or offensive purposes
  • Send sensitive information via unsecured channels (standard unencrypted email)
  • Use personal email, WhatsApp, Google Drive, or Dropbox for company data
  • Connect unapproved USB devices to company systems — USB access requires IT approval
  • Attempt to bypass any security control — electronic, physical, or otherwise
  • Develop or procure technology solutions without prior IT approval
A·3 Data Privacy — 7 Principles ▼

Premas handles personal data from employees, customers, partners, and research subjects. Non-compliance with data privacy obligations can lead to significant legal penalties. Follow these principles:

  • Purpose: Store data only for legitimate, defined purposes
  • Security: Keep data safe — treat others' data as you would your own
  • Retention: Keep data only as long as necessary and legally permissible
  • Usage: Be mindful of how data is collected, used, and retained in daily operations
  • Accuracy: Maintain accurate and up-to-date data at all times
  • Transparency: Clearly communicate how personal data is used
  • Reporting: Report any data loss, theft, or unauthorised disclosure to the InfoSec team immediately
Confidential information — including salary details, pricing, client data, and research results — must be shared only with those who need to know. Use an NDA when sharing confidential information externally.
A·4 Communications & Social Media Policy ▼

External communications: Only designated employees may speak to media, respond to corporate enquiries, or post on behalf of Premas. All media enquiries — including from journalists, analysts, or investors — must be forwarded to the BD team immediately without personal comment.

Social media:
  • Only designated employees may post on company social media accounts
  • Do not open social media accounts in the company's name or use its brands without permission
  • Personal social media use must not damage Premas' reputation or be used to bully, harass, or cause distress to any person
  • Never share confidential company information on any social platform
  • Violations of the social media policy are treated seriously and may lead to disciplinary action
Section 4

Environment, Health, Safety & Human Rights

E·1 EHS — Zero Accidents Goal ▼

Premas is dedicated to a safe and healthy workplace. Our EHS programme is built on one principle: all accidents are preventable.

  • Follow all safety protocols, SOPs, and PPE requirements without exception
  • Report hazards, near-misses, and incidents immediately to your manager or the EHS team
  • Participate in all safety training — including mandatory annual training
  • All products must comply with national and international transport and packaging regulations
  • Sustainability: minimise waste, conserve resources, and consider environmental impact in daily decisions
E·2 Human Rights & Modern Slavery ▼

Modern slavery — which includes forced labour, underage workers, exploitation, and unacceptable working conditions — is a crime and a violation of fundamental human rights. Premas has zero tolerance for any form of modern slavery in our operations or supply chain.

  • We work exclusively with individuals who freely choose to work and have equal rights
  • We will not engage with suppliers or organisations that use child labour, forced labour, or deny freedom of association
  • If you suspect an instance of modern slavery anywhere in our supply chain, report it to your line manager immediately
E·3 Working With Partners & Suppliers ▼

Premas' suppliers and third-party providers must adhere to all applicable laws and regulations. Our Ethical Sourcing Policy requires compliance with:

  • Anti-Bribery and Corruption standards
  • Modern Slavery and Trafficking prohibition
  • Competition and Anti-Trust laws
  • Environment, Health & Safety regulations
  • Product Safety and Transport Compliance
Section 5

How to Raise a Concern

If you witness illegal acts, improper conduct, or any breach of this Code — you have a duty to report it. Raising concerns in good faith protects you from retaliation. Silence is never the right answer.

👤

Step 1 — Your Line Manager

For most concerns, your first point of contact. Speak directly. If your manager is involved in the concern, skip to Step 2.

👥

Step 2 — HR Team or Head HR

For concerns involving your manager, sensitive people matters, or if you are uncomfortable speaking with your team.

🔒

Step 3 — Anonymous Portal

For serious legal matters or where you wish to remain anonymous. Independent senior management handles reports, with the Board overseeing all submissions.
