Tenure & Exit

Probation, Notice & Confirmation

Your full employment journey — from Day 1 training period through probation, confirmation, and the notice period rules that govern how either side exits the relationship.

Your Tenure Journey

From Day 1 to confirmed employee.

Your employment at Premas follows a defined progression from joining to confirmation. Understanding each phase — what is expected, what you're entitled to, and what the rules are — protects both you and the company.

Days 1 – 30
Training Period

Your first 30 days are formally a training period. If you resign during this window, you will be paid only Basic Salary for the days worked. For any unserved portion of the 30-day notice, you will be required to pay the equivalent basic salary amount to the company.

Months 1 – 6
Probationary Period

Standard probation is 6 months from your date of joining. During this period the notice period is 1 month on either side. EL cannot be availed; taking leave during probation may extend it. You are expected to demonstrate performance, conduct, and alignment with Premas values.

Month 6
Confirmation Review

At the end of 6 months, your performance and conduct are formally evaluated. You may be required to present your learnings and achievements to an assessment panel. Successful evaluation leads to confirmation as a permanent employee.

Post-Confirmation
Permanent Employment

Upon confirmation, notice period increases to 3 months on either side. All leave entitlements become fully active. You are now eligible for the annual appraisal cycle (AADP).

Age 60
Retirement

Premas' standard retirement age is 60. Continuation beyond this age is at the company's sole discretion, based on organisational needs and continued satisfactory performance.

If Probation Is Extended

When confirmation doesn't come immediately.

If your performance or conduct during probation does not fully meet Premas standards, your probation may be extended rather than terminated immediately. The extension follows a structured sequence:

Notice Period — Full Details

How exits work — yours and ours.

The notice period is a mutual protection mechanism — it gives the company time to plan for your departure and gives you time to hand over properly. Understanding the rules in detail prevents misunderstandings.

N·1 Post-Confirmation — 3 Months ▼

Once confirmed, either party may terminate the employment by providing 3 months' written notice. The company will determine how the notice period is served and whether any portion may be waived.

  • If you do not serve the full notice, the company may recover pro-rata basic salary for unserved days
  • The company's decision on notice period service is final — minimising business disruption is the priority
  • Notice must be given in writing
N·2 Physical Presence Is Mandatory During Notice ▼

Continuous physical presence on the job is required during the notice period. For calculating days served:

  • Saturdays, Sundays, and company-declared holidays count towards notice days served
  • Earned Leave (EL), Sick Leave (SL), and Casual Leave (CL) do NOT count as notice days
  • Any leave taken during notice extends the notice period by the equivalent number of days
  • Previously approved leave may be automatically revoked once notice is served
N·3 Waiver, Buyout & Shortfall ▼

Notice period waiver or payment in lieu requires approval from the competent authority in consultation with HR. It is not automatic.

  • Company may accept payment in lieu of notice from either side — this requires formal approval
  • Any shortfall in notice served without approval will be recovered from final settlement
  • Deductions for incomplete notice may be made from any dues owed to you at exit
N·4 Notice Period Changes ▼

Promotions, job-grade changes, or changes in business requirements may lead to immediate modifications to your notice period. These are governed by current company policies, are binding, and will be communicated to you promptly.

N·5 Early Resignation — Cost Recovery ▼

If you resign for any reason within specific time thresholds, Premas will recover costs incurred on your behalf:

  • Resignation within first 12 months: 100% of costs incurred recovered — relocation expenses, travel reimbursements, notice buyouts, or any other costs borne by the company on joining
  • Resignation within the second year: 50% of such costs recovered

This is recoverable from your final settlement dues.

N·6 Zero Tolerance — Immediate Termination ▼

In cases of zero-tolerance offences, your services may be terminated without any notice, regardless of whether you are on probation or confirmed. Zero-tolerance offences include:

  • Physical or mental incapacity rendering you unable to perform duties
  • Insubordination — wilful refusal to follow reasonable instructions
  • Dishonesty — any form of deception, falsification of records, or fraud
  • Unauthorised absence — absconding without communication
  • Data tampering or breach of confidentiality obligations
  • Harassment, bullying, or workplace misconduct
  • Any other serious misconduct as determined by management
There is no warning before zero-tolerance termination. These clauses exist to protect the company, its IP, and every person who works here.
Appraisal System

How performance is measured and rewarded.

Premas uses the Annual Appraisal and Development Programme (AADP) to evaluate performance, identify development needs, and determine increments and promotions. Understanding the system helps you participate effectively and plan your growth.

Appraisal Cycle Structure

Evaluation Period1st April to 31st March
Q1April – June
Q2July – September
Q3October – December
Q4January – March
Increments & PromotionsJuly (post annual review)

How It Works

  • KRAs (Key Result Areas) are set with your manager at the start of each quarter
  • KRAs are evaluated at the end of each quarter
  • Training needs are identified as part of the AADP process
  • Annual review synthesises all four quarters
  • Eligibility: you must have joined on or before 30 September to be eligible for that year's AADP
  • If you join after 30 September, your first full AADP cycle is the following year
  • Pro-rata appraisal applies if you joined before 30 September mid-cycle
You must complete all appraisal documentation to be eligible for an increment or promotion. Incomplete forms, missed KRA submissions, or failure to participate in the quarterly review process will affect your eligibility. The appraisal process is your responsibility as much as your manager's.
