Departments & Org Structure

Every team at Premas, what they do, who they work with, and how the company fits together. Knowing this map helps you understand where you sit — and who you'll rely on.

How We're Organised

A science-first company with integrated functions.

Premas is structured around its core CDMO mission: develop and manufacture biologics for partners. Every department exists to support that mission — either by doing the science directly, enabling the science operationally, or protecting the quality and integrity of everything we produce.

At the top sits the Founder & Chairman and the Managing Director, who jointly own company strategy and external representation. Reporting to them are the functional heads of each department — operating with significant autonomy but tightly aligned on quality standards, timelines, and client commitments.

What "cross-functional" means at Premas: Because we operate as a CDMO, most programmes require multiple departments working in parallel — R&D designs the process, Manufacturing executes it, QA validates it, Regulatory packages it, and BD manages the client relationship. You will work with colleagues outside your team from Day 1.

Department 01

Research & Development (R&D)

R&D is the engine of Premas. It is where the three proprietary platforms — D-CRYPT™, AXTEX-4D™, and C-QWENCE™ — were born, and where they continue to evolve. R&D is also responsible for delivering the scientific work for every partner programme: developing the molecular biology, cell biology, and process science that underpins each client engagement.

R&D · 1 Molecular Biology (Mol Bio) ▼

The foundational science layer. Mol Bio covers gene design and synthesis, vector construction, cloning strategies, and the creation of expression constructs for every protein target. This is where the molecular blueprint for a D-CRYPT™ expression run is created.


Day-to-day work includes:
  • Gene synthesis and codon optimisation for yeast expression
  • Vector design, restriction cloning, and Gibson assembly
  • Transformation, colony screening, and construct verification
  • Small-scale expression screening of candidate constructs
  • Maintenance of strain libraries and sequence databases

Interfaces with: Cell Biology (for expression testing), Process Development (for scale-up handoff), QA (for documentation of constructs used in GMP runs).
R&D · 2 Cell Biology ▼

Cell Biology handles the living systems — from yeast and mammalian host cell culture, to the AXTEX-4D™ 3D tissueoid platform, to cell line development for partner programmes. This team translates Mol Bio constructs into actual protein expression.


Day-to-day work includes:
  • Host cell maintenance (yeast, CHO, HEK293, insect cells)
  • Stable cell line development and banking
  • Protein expression screening at small scale
  • AXTEX-4D™ 3D culture model construction and maintenance
  • Drug sensitivity assays on 3D tumour models
  • Cell viability, morphology, and growth characterisation

Interfaces with: Mol Bio (receives constructs), Process Development (hands off optimised cell lines), Analytical (provides samples for characterisation).
R&D · 3 Process Development ▼

Process Development (PD) takes a cell line expressing a target protein and builds a robust, scalable, reproducible manufacturing process around it. This team bridges science and manufacturing — designing the upstream (fermentation/bioreactor) and downstream (purification) steps that will eventually be transferred to cGMP Manufacturing.


Day-to-day work includes:
  • Upstream process design: media optimisation, induction conditions, bioreactor parameters
  • Downstream development: chromatography, filtration, formulation
  • Process robustness and design-of-experiment (DoE) studies
  • Scale-up from shake flasks → bench bioreactors → pilot scale
  • Process transfer documentation for Manufacturing handoff

Interfaces with: Cell Biology (receives cell lines), Manufacturing (process transfer), Analytical (in-process analytics), Regulatory (process documentation for submissions).
R&D · 4 Analytical Development & Functional Assays ▼

Analytical Development ensures every protein produced by Premas can be fully characterised, tested, and documented to regulatory standards. This team develops and validates the methods that prove a protein is what it is, that it's pure, active, and stable.


Day-to-day work includes:
  • Method development: HPLC, SDS-PAGE, Western blot, SEC, IEF, CE
  • Identity assays: mass spectrometry, peptide mapping, N-terminal sequencing
  • Potency / functional assays: bioassays, ELISA, cell-based assays
  • Stability studies (ICH-compliant) for clinical programmes
  • Method validation packages for IND/NDA submissions

Interfaces with: Process Development (in-process analytics), QC (method transfer for batch release), Regulatory (method validation documentation).
Department 02

Manufacturing & cGMP Operations

Manufacturing is where processes designed by R&D are executed at scale under current Good Manufacturing Practice (cGMP) conditions. Every batch produced here generates documentation that can support regulatory submissions to the US FDA, EMA, or CDSCO. This team operates with the discipline and rigour of a regulated environment — because the proteins they make end up in clinical trials.

What Manufacturing Does

  • Executes fermentation and bioreactor runs per validated process
  • Downstream purification: chromatography columns, TFF, filtration
  • Formulation and filling of final protein product
  • Batch record completion and deviation investigation
  • Equipment qualification, calibration, and maintenance
  • Facility management: cleanroom upkeep, environmental monitoring

cGMP — What It Means

cGMP (current Good Manufacturing Practice) is the set of regulations enforced by the US FDA, EMA, and other agencies to ensure drugs are consistently produced and controlled according to quality standards. At Premas, every cGMP batch comes with a full batch record, a Certificate of Analysis, and documentation that can be submitted directly to regulators. This is what makes Premas' output valuable — not just the protein, but the documented proof of how it was made.

If you work near or in Manufacturing: cGMP areas have strict access controls, gowning requirements, and documentation protocols. You will be trained specifically on these before you enter. No exceptions — this protects the integrity of every batch and the safety of every patient downstream.
Department 03

Quality Assurance & Quality Control

QA and QC are the guardians of Premas' reputation. In a regulated CDMO environment, quality is not a checkbox — it is the entire foundation on which client trust and regulatory approval rest. While they work closely together, QA and QC have distinct mandates.

🛡

Quality Assurance (QA)

Process-level: QA designs, implements, and maintains the quality management system (QMS). They write and own SOPs, manage deviations and CAPA (Corrective and Preventive Action), oversee change control, manage internal and external audits, and ensure the entire facility operates in a state of compliance. QA approves batches for release.

🔬

Quality Control (QC)

Product-level: QC tests every batch against the approved specification before it leaves the facility. They run release assays — purity, identity, potency, sterility, endotoxin — and generate the Certificate of Analysis that accompanies every shipment. QC is the last scientific check before a product reaches a client or enters a clinical trial.

Why QA/QC matters for everyone: Whether you're in R&D, Manufacturing, or a support function — any documentation you create, any sample you handle, any process you touch may eventually fall under QA review. Following procedures accurately, documenting everything, and escalating deviations immediately is everyone's responsibility. It is never just "QA's problem."
Department 04

Regulatory Affairs

Regulatory Affairs (RA) is the bridge between Premas' scientific work and the agencies that must approve it. Every clinical trial, every batch of cGMP material, every new therapeutic needs regulatory clearance — and the RA team manages that interface on behalf of Premas and its partners.

📄

IND / CTA Submissions

Compiling and submitting Investigational New Drug (IND) applications to the US FDA and Clinical Trial Authorisations (CTAs) to the EMA and national agencies for partner programmes.

🌍

Global Regulatory Intelligence

Tracking regulatory changes across FDA, EMA, CDSCO, PMDA (Japan), and TGA (Australia) — ensuring Premas' processes and documentation remain compliant as rules evolve.

🤝

Agency Interactions

Managing pre-IND meetings, responding to agency queries, and representing Premas in formal regulatory proceedings. Fluent in agency expectations and language.

Export Control: Regulatory Affairs also manages compliance with technology transfer laws — including Indian export regulations and, for US programmes, EAR (Export Administration Regulations) and ITAR. As an employee, you confirm compliance with these laws upon joining.

The Big Picture

How departments work together.

Understanding how a partner programme flows through Premas helps you see how your work connects to the bigger mission. Here is a simplified view of a typical D-CRYPT™ programme journey:

Step 1
BD signs the client — R&D receives the brief

BD closes the contract. The programme brief — target protein, therapeutic context, development milestones — is handed to R&D's Mol Bio team to begin construct design.

Step 2
Mol Bio designs the construct → Cell Biology expresses it

Gene design, vector construction, and cloning. Transformed into yeast host. Cell Biology screens expression levels, selects the best clone, and begins optimising growth.

Step 3
Process Development builds and scales the process

PD takes the optimised clone and designs the full upstream and downstream process. DoE studies establish robust parameters. Process is scaled to pilot bioreactor.

Step 4
Analytical validates the methods

Analytical Dev develops and validates all release and characterisation assays. These methods are transferred to QC and included in regulatory documentation.

Step 5
Manufacturing produces the cGMP batch

Process transferred. Manufacturing runs the batch under cGMP. Every step is documented in the batch record. QA oversees the process and approves the batch.

Step 6
QC releases, Regulatory packages, BD delivers

QC tests the batch and issues a Certificate of Analysis. RA includes the batch data in the IND/CTA submission package. BD delivers to the client — who takes it into clinical trial.

Your role in this chain: Regardless of your department, you are one link in a chain that ends with a patient in a clinical trial. That is the weight — and the privilege — of working at a CDMO. Every data point you generate, every SOP you follow, every deviation you document matters.
