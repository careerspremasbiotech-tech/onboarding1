The Career Framework

Why Premas built a structured career ladder.

In July 2025, Premas formally introduced a Structured Career Ladder Framework — a single, unified grading system that applies across all functions. Before this framework, role levels were fragmented and function-specific. The ladder changes that: it creates one shared language for career progression, from a Trainee joining R&D to the Managing Director.

🗺

Unified Structure

Levels 0–10-I apply across R&D, Manufacturing, Engineering, QA, HR, Finance, and BD. One framework, no function-specific inconsistency.

🔭

Scope-Based Placement

Your level reflects the actual complexity, decision-making scope, and impact of your role — not just years on your CV. Two people with 12 years of experience may sit at different levels.

📈

Growth Visibility

Typical experience ranges and ideal time-in-band are defined for every level. You can see the path ahead — and understand what it takes to move along it.

⚖️

Fair Promotions

Promotions are anchored in demonstrated capability, scope, and contribution — not tenure alone. This protects internal equity and removes arbitrariness from advancement decisions.

"The role you're given reflects what the company needs from you. We'll back you — but you have to deliver consistently. Accountability is on you to operate at your level fully, in decision-making, output, and ownership."
— Premas Biotech, Career Ladder Philosophy
Three Terms — Explained

Grade, Band, and Designation — how they differ.

🔢

Grade

A numeric level (e.g. Level 7-I, Level 8-II). The internal calibration reference — used by HR for compensation benchmarking, promotion decisions, and workforce planning. May not be visible externally.

🏷

Band

A grouping of adjacent levels with similar scope and seniority. Bands indicate the broad layer you sit in — Execution, Professional, Management, Leadership, or Executive. Used for peer comparison across functions.

💼

Designation

Your formal job title (e.g. Trainee, Executive, Senior Manager, VP). The title that appears on your business card and appointment letter. Reflects your level in a way that is meaningful externally.

PhD Equivalence: At Premas, a PhD is treated as approximately 5 years of research experience. Most PhD hires begin at Assistant Manager (Level 7-I) or higher — in line with biotech industry norms globally.

Scope Evaluation

What determines your band — beyond years.

Placement in a band is determined by the actual scope of your role — not just your experience. These 9 parameters are the lens through which every placement and promotion decision is evaluated:

ParameterWhat Is Evaluated
Key ResponsibilitiesNature of work delivered — operational, strategic, support, or leadership
Role ComplexityDegree of ambiguity, ownership, and coordination involved in daily deliverables
Decision-Making ScopeTypes of decisions made and their team-level / function-level / org-wide impact
Team & People LeadershipResponsibility for direct/indirect reports, mentoring, and team growth
Cross-Functional ExposureDegree of active collaboration across different functions or business units
External InterfaceDepth of interaction with customers, partners, auditors, vendors, or regulators
Strategic InfluenceContribution to planning, policy formation, and long-term initiatives
Execution MaturityDepth of thinking, risk anticipation, and problem-solving under pressure
System-Setting CapabilityCapacity to define new SOPs, ways of working, or institutional practices
Illustrative Example: Two professionals both have 12 years of experience. One leads a 20-member team, manages a departmental budget, and drives cross-functional programmes → placed as General Manager. The other is a deep technical individual contributor with no team or budget accountability → may remain at Manager or Deputy Manager. Role scope — not years — determines placement.
Organisational Beliefs

The four pillars of Premas culture.

These are not aspirational statements — they are the standards we hold ourselves to every day. Every team member is expected to embody these beliefs in how they work, communicate, and make decisions.

Belief 01

Leading-Edge

  • Always working on developing newer technologies to add to our products
  • Adopting best practices across all areas of operations
  • Continuously working towards upgrading team potential
  • Staying open to new ideas at all times
Belief 02

Performance Driven

  • Always working with each other to deliver on customer needs
  • Working efficiently, earnestly, and meticulously
  • Respecting time and processes
  • Growing together — not at each other's expense
Belief 03

Ethical

  • Being transparent in all our dealings — maintaining integrity
  • Being ethical to customers, investors, employees, associates, and community
  • Respecting confidentiality, data legitimacy, and IP protection
  • Zero tolerance for unethical practices
Belief 04

Obsessed With Customer Experience

  • Listening actively and creating bespoke solutions
  • Continuously innovating based on customer needs
  • Being responsive and delivering on time
  • Building long-term relationships — not transactional ones
