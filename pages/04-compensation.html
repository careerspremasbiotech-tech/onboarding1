Compensation & Benefits

A complete breakdown of how your CTC is built — every component, every deduction, every statutory benefit explained clearly. Your pay is confidential; this module helps you understand it fully.

Confidentiality First

Your salary is confidential.

Your remuneration is based on your qualifications and professional experience. You are required to keep the terms of your remuneration — including any future changes — strictly confidential. Unauthorised disclosure of your salary details to colleagues, external parties, or anyone other than the HR department will be considered a breach of confidentiality and may result in immediate disciplinary action.

This applies both ways: Do not share your salary with others, and do not ask or pressure colleagues to share theirs. Compensation is a private matter between you and Premas.
CTC Structure

How your cost-to-company is built.

Your CTC (Cost to Company) is the total annual cost Premas bears for your employment. It is made up of two parts: what you receive directly (Gross Salary) and what the company contributes on your behalf (Statutory Employer Contributions). The specific amounts are detailed in Annexure A of your Appointment Letter.

ComponentTypeWhat It Is
Basic Salary Gross Component The foundation of your pay. Used as the base for PF and Gratuity calculations. Typically ~50% of Gross.
House Rent Allowance (HRA) Gross Component 50% of Basic Salary. Partially tax-exempt if you live in a rented property and submit rent receipts.
Special Allowance Gross Component The balance component — makes up the remainder of your Gross Salary above Basic and HRA. Fully taxable.
Statutory Bonus Gross Component Mandated under the Payment of Bonus Act. Calculated on a statutory basis. Included in Gross.
Gross Salary (A) Total Gross Basic + HRA + Special Allowance + Statutory Bonus = Your Gross Salary
PF — Employer Contribution Employer Add-on 12% of Basic (capped at ₹1,800/month). Company's statutory contribution to your Provident Fund — paid over and above your Gross.
Labour Welfare Fund (LWF) Employer Add-on Statutory employer contribution to LWF — a small amount mandated by state law.
Gratuity Provision Employer Add-on Monthly provision for gratuity liability — payable to you after 5 years of continuous service. Included in CTC as a provisioned amount.
Total CTC (A + B) Your CTC Gross Salary + All Employer Contributions = Total Cost to Company
Important: Only the salary schemes explicitly communicated in your Appointment Letter apply to you. No verbal commitments, informal discussions, or comparison with colleagues' packages create any entitlement. If you are uncertain about any component, speak only to the HR department.

Tax Obligations

TDS and your responsibility.

You are personally responsible for fulfilling all income tax obligations related to your remuneration. Premas is legally required to deduct Tax at Source (TDS) from your salary and will provide all necessary documents (Form 16, Form 12BB) for your tax filing.

Tax 1 Investment Declaration — what you must do ▼

At the start of each financial year (April), you are required to submit an Investment Declaration — a statement of all investments you plan to make during the year that qualify for tax deduction under Chapter VI-A (80C, 80D, HRA, etc.).

  • Declarations must be submitted in the format and on the timeline specified by HR/Finance
  • Failure to submit means TDS will be deducted at the maximum applicable rate
  • At year-end (February–March), you must submit actual proof of investments made
  • Discrepancies between declaration and actual proof will result in TDS adjustment
Submit your declaration early. Late declarations cannot be backdated and will result in higher TDS for the months already processed.
Tax 2 Old Regime vs New Regime — your choice ▼

Under current Indian income tax law, you can choose between the Old Tax Regime (with deductions under 80C, HRA, etc.) or the New Tax Regime (lower slab rates but fewer deductions). You must communicate your regime choice to HR at the start of the financial year. This affects how TDS is computed.

  • Old Regime: Allows deductions for PF, HRA, LIC, home loan, etc. Better if you have significant investments.
  • New Regime: Lower slab rates, no deductions. Better if you have few deductions.
  • Consult a tax advisor if you are unsure which regime is more beneficial for your situation.
Tax 3 Statutory deductions you cannot opt out of ▼

Certain deductions are mandated by law and are not optional:

  • PF (₹1,800/month): Mandatory if your Basic is above a threshold. Your account is portable — you can transfer it from a previous employer using your UAN.
  • ESI (if applicable): Mandatory if your Gross is within the ESI wage ceiling. Provides medical insurance and sickness benefits.
  • LWF: Small statutory deduction mandated by Haryana state rules.
Benefits

What Premas provides beyond your salary.

🏥

Medical Insurance — ₹5,00,000 Cover

Group health insurance covering you, your spouse, and up to two dependent children. Cover of ₹5 lakh per annum per insured person. Cashless hospitalisation at network hospitals. Details of the policy and network hospitals will be shared by HR.

🛡

Accidental Insurance

Personal accident cover as per company policy. Covers accidental death, permanent total disability, and permanent partial disability. Policy details shared separately.

⏳

Gratuity — After 5 Years

You become eligible for gratuity after completing 5 years of continuous service at Premas. Calculated as 15 days' Basic Salary per year of service, payable at the time of separation (resignation, retirement, or death/disability).

📱

Provident Fund — Retirement Savings

Your PF account accumulates throughout your employment — your ₹1,800/month plus the employer's matching ₹1,800/month. This is a powerful long-term savings vehicle. You can check your balance via the EPFO portal using your UAN.

Benefits during Notice Period: Upon commencement of your notice period, no additional benefits or new schemes will apply. Benefits continue as-is until your last working day.
